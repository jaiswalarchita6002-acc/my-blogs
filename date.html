LOVE NOWADAYS

BLOGS by ARCHITA JAISWAL – 5 December 2025

Love nowadays… honestly, what even is it? Because sometimes it feels like we’ve drifted so far from the actual meaning that we’re just pretending we know what we’re doing. Real love used to feel simple — not easy, but simple. It meant showing up, talking things out, staying even when it was inconvenient. It meant effort, not excuses. But somewhere between “seen” and “typing…”, we lost that softness. Now love feels like a glitchy update that everyone got but no one knows how to use properly.

We keep saying we want something real, something genuine, something peaceful — yet we’re the same people running away the second things start feeling too real. It’s like we want depth but only in shallow doses. We want attachment, but only if the other person catches feelings first. We crave love but we’re so terrified of heartbreak that we’d rather stay in a grey zone than actually commit. And that’s where today’s dating culture gets messy — because instead of clarity, we’ve built an entire relationship structure out of confusion.

And it’s crazy how naturally we’ve adopted all these modern dating phases like they’re part of some national syllabus. Talking stage, situationship, almost relationship… everything feels like something but nothing is actually anything. You talk to someone daily, laugh, flirt, share memes, open up about things you’ve never told anyone — and still, no one knows what it is. Ask “so what are we?” and suddenly the entire planet goes silent. People disappear mid-conversation like they’ve been teleported to another dimension. Dry texting has become some sort of passive-aggressive communication style. Soft launching has replaced actually introducing someone. And emotional games? God. Everyone wants loyalty, but no one wants to say it aloud. Everyone wants effort, but no one wants to be “too much.” And we’re all caught in this endless, exhausting loop of decoding someone’s emojis like it’s rocket science.

But nothing quite exposes the chaos of modern love like dating apps do. Because dating apps sell you this illusion — that finding your person is just a swipe away, but most of the time, you just end up scrolling through a catalogue of people who don’t know what they want. You match, talk for two days, lose interest, match with someone else, repeat. It’s like window shopping for emotions. The abundance of choices makes everyone disposable, like if one thing feels difficult, we just move to the next match instead of actually trying. And with so many choices, everyone feels replaceable… which somehow makes everyone feel lonelier.

And yet, despite all this chaos, we still fall. We still hope. We still wait for that one person who texts with intention, shows up without being asked, makes love feel less like a battlefield and more like breathing again. Maybe love isn’t gone — maybe it's just harder to notice in a world where everything moves so fast and everyone is scared of slowing down. Maybe we haven’t lost the essence of love… maybe we’re just learning, painfully and slowly, how to find it again in a generation that confuses attention with affection and consistency with pressure.

Love nowadays is confusing, unpredictable, sometimes disappointing — but somewhere in this mess, there’s still a part of us that believes something real will find us. And maybe that tiny piece of hope… is what keeps all of us trying.

“Love hasn’t vanished; it’s just waiting for eyes that still know how to see it.”
