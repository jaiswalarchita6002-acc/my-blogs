College-mates or Critics?

BLOGS by ARCHITA JAISWAL — JAN 08, 2026

People in College… are they okay? Because I have questions.

Because why does it feel like I came here to study and everyone else came here to conduct social experiments? Every day I discover a new species of human behavior that I was not warned about in the admission brochure. And the funny part is, nobody thinks they’re dramatic. Everyone thinks they’re normal. Meanwhile the rest of us are just trying to survive lectures and unnecessary nonsense at the same time.

First of all, why are some people so emotionally invested in other people’s lives? Like they know who you talk to, how often you talk, and somehow have opinions about it too. I barely remember what I ate yesterday, but some people are maintaining full spreadsheets of everyone’s social activity. And for what? Research? Final year project? Because the dedication is honestly impressive.

Then there are the people who always have something to say about your appearance. If you dress up, it’s “trying too hard.” If you don’t, it’s “why are you so low effort today?” If you’re confident, you’re arrogant. If you’re quiet, you’re boring. At this point I feel like there’s a secret rulebook about how to exist and nobody gave me my copy. Or maybe the rules just change based on who’s judging that day.

And don’t even get me started on the double-faced professionals. Sweet to your face, commentary behind your back. In front of you it’s all smiles and small talk, and the moment you leave the room it turns into a full discussion panel. Like if you don’t like someone, at least be consistent about it. This premium two-in-one personality pack is not necessary.

Also, why is gossip treated like a group activity? People will say “I don’t like drama” and then proceed to deliver a detailed episode recap of everyone else’s business. With analysis. With opinions. With future predictions. At this point it’s not gossip, it’s a podcast.

Honestly, college sometimes feels less like an educational institution and more like a daily test of patience. You learn about assignments, deadlines, and also which conversations to avoid, which people to keep at a safe distance, and how to pretend you didn’t hear something extremely unnecessary.

But somehow, in between all this chaos, you still find a few normal people. The ones who mind their own business, laugh at stupid things, and don’t treat every interaction like a strategic move. And those people? They make the whole experience slightly less exhausting.

So yeah, people in college are confusing, entertaining, annoying, and sometimes way too involved in things that do not concern them. But I guess that’s part of the experience. You come for the degree, you stay for the unplanned reality show.

“I enrolled for education, not emotional side quests… but here we are.”
